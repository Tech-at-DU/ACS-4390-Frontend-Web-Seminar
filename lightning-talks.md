

# Lightning Talks — Expectations

Lightning talks are short presentations where you introduce a frontend technology or concept to the class. They are a core part of this seminar and help the class explore the **breadth of the frontend ecosystem**.

---

## What is a Lightning Talk?

A lightning talk is a **5–8 minute explanation** of a tool, framework, or architectural idea.

Your goal is not to explain everything. Your goal is to help the class understand:

- what the technology is
- why it exists
- how it works at a high level
- what its strengths and weaknesses are

---

## What to Cover

A strong lightning talk answers these four questions:

1. **What problem does this technology solve?**
2. **How does it work?** (high-level explanation)
3. **What are its advantages?**
4. **What are the trade-offs or limitations?**

You do not need to go into deep implementation details.

---

## Choosing a Topic

Each week you will choose a topic from the **weekly topics list**.

Guidelines:

- Choose something you are curious about
- Choose something you can explain clearly in a few minutes
- Avoid choosing your **term project topic**

If a topic seems broad (e.g. "Single Page Applications"), focus on one angle such as:

- What is it?
- Why does it exist?
- What problems does it solve?
- How does it work?
- What are the trade-offs?

---

## Preparation

At the beginning of class you will have time to prepare your talk.

You may use:

- short notes
- a few slides (optional)
- a simple example or diagram

Keep preparation lightweight. Focus on clarity, not polish.

---

## During Your Talk

- Speak clearly and stay focused
- Keep your explanation simple
- Avoid reading directly from slides
- Be ready to answer a few questions

Your talk should help the class quickly understand the idea.

---

## What Makes a Good Lightning Talk?

Good lightning talks:

- focus on **why the technology exists**
- explain **trade-offs**, not just features
- use simple examples
- stay within time limits

Weaker talks tend to:

- list features without explanation
- repeat documentation
- try to cover too much

---

## Quick Outline (Optional)

If you want a simple structure, use this 4–slide outline:

1. **Problem** — What issue does this solve?
2. **Approach** — How does it work (high level)?
3. **Pros** — Why is it useful?
4. **Cons** — When should you avoid it?

---

## Key Idea

A lightning talk is not about being an expert.

It is about helping others understand:

> Why does this technology exist, and when should we use it?

If your talk answers that clearly, it is successful.
