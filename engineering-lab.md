

# Engineering Lab — Expectations

The Engineering Lab (Thursday sessions) is where you make progress on your **term project** through experimentation, building, and discussion.

This is not a lecture. It is a **working session** where you actively develop ideas and test solutions.

---

## What Happens in the Engineering Lab

During lab sessions, students will:

- demonstrate prototypes or experiments
- share code (even if incomplete)
- discuss technical challenges
- get feedback from the group
- plan next steps for their project

The goal is to connect ideas from seminar discussions to **real engineering work**.

---

## What You Should Bring Each Week

You are expected to come prepared with **something to show or discuss**.

This can include:

- a working prototype or demo
- partial or incomplete code
- results from testing or measurements
- a comparison between tools or approaches
- specific technical questions

If your work is incomplete, that is okay. In fact, sharing unfinished work is encouraged.

---

## Examples of Good Contributions

- "I compared Vite and Webpack startup times — here are the results"
- "Here’s a demo I built, but I’m stuck on this issue"
- "I tried two approaches and here are the trade-offs"

Good contributions focus on **real work, real problems, and real decisions**.

---

## Expectations for Participation

Each student should contribute during every lab session by:

- sharing progress
- asking questions
- giving feedback to others

Because this is a small class, active participation is essential.

---

## By the End of Each Lab

By the end of each session, you should have a clear idea of:

- what you accomplished
- what problems you encountered
- what you will work on next

This helps ensure steady progress on your term project.

---

## Key Idea

The Engineering Lab is where your project becomes real.

It is not about having a perfect solution. It is about:

> testing ideas, learning from results, and improving your approach.

If you are consistently building, testing, and asking questions, you are doing the lab correctly.
