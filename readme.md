# ACS 4390 - Front-End Technology Seminar

## Course Description

This course explores emerging ideas, tools, and architectural patterns in modern front-end web development. Students will research a focused topic related to front-end technology and develop a deep understanding of the systems, design decisions, and trade-offs behind modern web applications.

Students will evaluate current tools, frameworks, and practices used by professional developers. The course emphasizes research, experimentation, discussion, and presentation rather than step-by-step tutorials.

Students will investigate topics such as front-end architecture, performance, developer tooling, accessibility, design systems, and AI-assisted development.

---

## Why You Should Know This

Front-end development evolves rapidly. Frameworks, tooling, and patterns change faster than most traditional programming disciplines.

Professional developers are expected not only to write code, but to evaluate technologies, understand trade-offs, and make architectural decisions.

This course develops the ability to:

- investigate new technologies
- analyze design decisions
- explain complex technical systems
- communicate ideas to other developers

These skills are essential for engineers who want to stay effective as the web ecosystem continues to evolve.

---

## Prerequisites

- ACS 1320 – JavaScript Foundations
- ACS 3330 – Single Page Applications

Students should be comfortable with:

- JavaScript
- modern frontend frameworks (React or similar)
- APIs and web application architecture

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, you will be able to:

1. Investigate and analyze modern front-end technologies
2. Explain architectural decisions behind real-world web applications
3. Evaluate tools and frameworks used in modern frontend development
4. Research and synthesize technical information from multiple sources
5. Design and present a technical topic to a developer audience
6. Build small experimental prototypes to test technical ideas
7. Critically evaluate emerging technologies such as AI-assisted development tools

## Research Topics

Students will choose a topic related to modern front-end development. Possible areas include:

- State management architectures
- React vs Web Components
- Front-end performance optimization
- Accessibility systems and tooling
- Design systems and component libraries
- Static site generation and server components
- Developer tooling and build systems
- CSS architecture and layout systems
- AI-assisted programming tools
- Testing strategies for frontend applications

Students will submit a proposal describing:

- their topic
- why it is relevant
- what questions they will investigate
- how they will demonstrate their findings

---

## Assignments

During the course of the class you will complete the following work:

### 1. Topic Proposal
Define the topic you will research during the seminar.

### 2. Annotated Research Sources
Collect and summarize articles, documentation, and other sources related to your topic.

### 3. Prototype / Demonstration
Build a small project, experiment, or demo illustrating your topic.

### 4. Final Presentation
Present your research findings to the class.

---

## Homework

Students will conduct research outside of class and document their findings.

Typical work may include:

- reading documentation
- studying open source projects
- experimenting with frameworks
- building small prototypes
- preparing presentations

Students should expect to spend time exploring unfamiliar technologies and documenting what they learn.

## Notes for Improvement

### 1. The schedule is vague

Seminars easily drift into unstructured discussions. Students benefit from clear weekly goals.

Possible improvements:

- weekly research check-ins
- short reading discussions
- mini-presentations

---

### 2. Topic selection will still cause friction

Students often:

- pick something too big
- pick something too shallow

Possible improvements:

- provide a curated list of 25–30 topics
- include examples of strong proposals

---

### 3. Prototype expectations need clarity

Students will ask:

> How big should the project be?

Without guidance some will build too much while others will build almost nothing.

Recommendation:

A prototype should take **6–12 hours of work** and demonstrate the core concept being researched.
