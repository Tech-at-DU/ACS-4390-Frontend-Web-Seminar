# Weekly Lightning Talk Topics

Each week in ACS 4390 we will explore a **theme in frontend engineering**. During the Monday seminar session, students will give **lightning talks** introducing technologies related to that theme.

A lightning talk is a **5–8 minute explanation** of a tool, framework, or architectural idea. The goal is not to cover every detail, but to help the class understand:

- what problem the technology solves
- how it works at a high level
- its strengths and weaknesses


Weekly lightning talk topics are meant to explore the **breadth of the frontend ecosystem**. These topics are separate from your **term project topic**, which should investigate one technology or question in greater depth.

## How to Approach a Lightning Talk Topic

Some of the topics listed below are **intentionally broad**. Your lightning talk does not need to explain *everything* about the topic. Instead, focus on **one clear angle** that helps the class understand the technology.

For example, if your topic is **Single Page Applications**, you might focus on one of these questions:

- What **is** a Single Page Application?
- **Why** do Single Page Applications exist?
- **What problems** do SPAs solve compared to traditional multi‑page websites?
- **How** does an SPA work technically?
- What are the **trade‑offs or limitations** of SPAs?

Similarly, if your topic is **Static Site Generation**, you might ask:

- What is Static Site Generation?
- Why do developers use Static Site Generation?
- How does SSG differ from SSR and SPAs?
- What performance benefits does SSG provide?

A good lightning talk usually focuses on answering **one or two key questions** clearly rather than trying to cover the entire topic.

A useful way to structure your thinking is to consider questions like:

- **What is it?** (definition)
- **Why does it exist?** (problem it solves)
- **How does it work?** (high‑level explanation)
- **When should engineers use it?** (use cases)
- **What are the trade‑offs?** (limitations or downsides)

Your goal is to help the class quickly understand **why the technology exists and how it fits into modern frontend development**.

# Choosing Topics

Students will choose lightning talk topics at the beginning of each week. Try to choose topics that:

- you are curious about
- relate to your term project
- introduce something new to the class

You do **not** need to be an expert on the topic. The goal of the lightning talk is to **teach the class what the technology is and why it exists**.

---

# Lightning Talk Structure

A good lightning talk answers four questions:

1. **What problem does this technology solve?**
2. **How does it work?**
3. **What are its advantages?**
4. **What are the trade‑offs or limitations?**

Your talk should be short, clear, and focused on helping the class understand the technology.
